Project 1: Health Saver Mobile Site

Questionnaires

  1. Purpose
      The healthy planner mobile site will help plan and give direction to people who want to get healthy but do not have the time to do it on their own. With the healthy planner, a weekly diet accompanied by recipes to your liking will be available for you when you hit the grocery store after work or don’t know what to eat for the day.
  2. User
      People trying to get healthy, care about fitness, and people too busy to plan out a meal schedule. Average to high income, female/male, age of 18 to late 40’s.
  3. Problem Being Solved
      Giving users a weekly schedule of suggested foods to eat for breakfast, lunch, dinner and snacks along with recipes filtered to their preference. Helps those who do not know what to each for the day but want to each healthy. Gives you healthy options without making you think you are on a diet.
  4. Competitors
      Fitness Pal, Weight Watchers, Jenny Craig, other calorie counting or recipe sites.
  5. User Actions
      User inputs info about themselves (name, age, weight, goals, etc.) and food preferences within respective healthy food groups.
  6. Features
      Customized meals, healthy meals planned out weekly and recipes to your liking.

Project 2: USPS Postal Social Redesign

Questionnaires

  1. What site will you redesign?
    • I will be redesigning USPS's Postal Social website. You can visit the site here Postal Social Site.
  2. Who visits your site?
    • USPS customers of all types.
  3. What do your visitors look for?
    • Tips and services offered by USPS.
  4. Are they able to find it? If not, why?
    • The information is easy to find with the organized grouping the current site has. For example, All the tips and services related to military can be found under the military navigation, which breaks down into sub-navigations.
  5. What does the current site do well?
    • The current site is organized well.
  6. What does the current site do poorly?
    • The current site is filled with links that break down into more links to give the customer what they are looking for. There is no other structure to it. It's as if the site is an encyclopedia.
  7. What are short-term or long-term goals that need to be considered in the redesign?
    • Short term: The new design should be visually appealing while keeping the same organization.
    • Long term: Increase USPS customer base and customer visit frequency for the site.
  8. How does the user interface need changing?
    • There are currently three levels to the site right now. The main navigation of groups, sub-navigation for each group, and material in each sub-group.
  9. What is the most important factor of the new site?
    • The navigation.
  10. What areas would you improve in the redesign?
    • Finding a solution to better navigate through the site without having to click a bunch on links.
  11. What features would you eliminate in the redesign?
    • The redundant amount of links.
  12. What features would you highlight in the redesign?
    • Clear navigation with more insight of what is included within each section than just a handful of link.
    • Description of Postal Social's purpose
    • Social media, if any
    • USPS business information (footer area)
